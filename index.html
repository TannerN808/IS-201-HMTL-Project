Hawai'i Island

The Big Island

Hawai'i Island is made up of 5 different volcanoes:

  1. Kilauea
    • The most active of the five
    • Located on the South-East
    • Home to famous Hawai'i Volcanoes National Park
  2. Mauna Kea
    • The tallest mountain in the world including below sea level
    • Located North-Center
    • Has some of the world's best observatories
  3. Mauna Loa
    • Largest active volcano in the world
    • Located South-Center
    • Erupts approximately every 40 years
  4. Hualalai
    • It's volcanic cones provide ash soil for the famous Kona coffee
    • Located West on the island
    • Third youngest of the volcanoes
  5. Kohala
    • Oldest volcano at one million years old
    • Located on the North side
    • Is totally extinct unlike its dormant or active neighbors
